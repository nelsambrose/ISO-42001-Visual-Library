# Principle-01 Fairness

Brief explanation:
Fairness in AI means that systems should treat people equitably and avoid producing biased or discriminatory outcomes. It requires organisations to consider how AI decisions may affect different groups and to take active steps to identify and reduce unfair bias throughout the AI lifecycle.

Where it fits:
Fairness supports responsible AI governance by ensuring that AI systems do not cause disproportionate harm or disadvantage. It underpins the human-centred values expected of a well-governed AI Management System.

Practical use:
- Define what fairness means in the context of each AI system before deployment
- Assess training data for potential sources of bias
- Test AI outputs across different demographic groups
- Document fairness criteria in AI system design records
- Review fairness considerations as part of regular performance evaluation

Evidence examples:
- Bias assessment reports completed during AI system development
- Fairness criteria documented in AI system design or scoping records
- Test results showing performance across different groups
- Records of fairness-related incidents and actions taken
- Policies referencing fairness as a design requirement

Caveat:
This is a learning aid and should be validated against the official ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard and relevant organisational obligations.
