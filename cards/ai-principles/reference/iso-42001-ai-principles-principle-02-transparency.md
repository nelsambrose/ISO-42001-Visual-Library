# Principle-02 Transparency

Brief explanation:
Transparency means being open about how AI systems work, what data they use, and how decisions are reached. Organisations should be able to explain AI behaviour in terms that are meaningful to the people affected by it, even when the underlying models are complex.

Where it fits:
Transparency supports responsible AI governance by building trust with stakeholders and enabling effective oversight. It is a core expectation within an AI Management System where accountability and explainability are required.

Practical use:
- Document the purpose, inputs, and outputs of each AI system
- Provide plain-language explanations of how AI decisions are made
- Disclose when individuals are interacting with or affected by an AI system
- Maintain records of model versions, training data sources, and known limitations
- Include transparency requirements in AI procurement and supplier agreements

Evidence examples:
- AI system documentation describing decision logic at an appropriate level
- User-facing notices or disclosures about AI use
- Model cards or factsheets for deployed AI systems
- Audit trails showing when and how AI decisions were made
- Supplier contracts including transparency obligations

Caveat:
This is a learning aid and should be validated against the official ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard and relevant organisational obligations.
