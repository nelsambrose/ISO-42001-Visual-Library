# Principle-03 Accountability

Brief explanation:
Accountability means that there are clear lines of responsibility for AI systems and their outcomes. Organisations should be able to identify who is responsible for each AI system at every stage of its lifecycle, from design and deployment through to decommissioning.

Where it fits:
Accountability underpins the governance structure of an AI Management System. It ensures that decisions about AI are made by identifiable people or roles, and that responsibility does not become diffuse or unassigned across teams.

Practical use:
- Assign named owners for each AI system across its lifecycle
- Define roles and responsibilities for AI governance in policy documents
- Establish escalation paths when AI systems cause harm or produce unexpected outputs
- Include accountability requirements in supplier and third-party agreements
- Review ownership assignments as part of regular AI system audits

Evidence examples:
- AI system ownership register with named responsible parties
- Role descriptions or policies defining AI accountability
- Incident records showing how responsibility was exercised when issues arose
- Meeting minutes or governance records showing accountability in practice
- Supplier contracts with defined AI accountability clauses

Caveat:
This is a learning aid and should be validated against the official ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard and relevant organisational obligations.
