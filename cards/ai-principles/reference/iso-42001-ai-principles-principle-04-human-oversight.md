# Principle-04 Human Oversight

Brief explanation:
Human oversight means that people retain meaningful control over AI systems and the decisions they influence. Organisations should design AI systems so that humans can intervene, correct, or override outputs, particularly in high-stakes contexts.

Where it fits:
Human oversight is central to responsible AI governance. It ensures that AI systems remain tools that support human judgement rather than replacing it without adequate safeguards, which is a key expectation within an AI Management System.

Practical use:
- Define the level of human review required for different categories of AI decision
- Build override and intervention mechanisms into AI system design
- Train staff on when and how to intervene in AI-assisted processes
- Document human oversight requirements in AI system specifications
- Monitor whether human oversight controls are being applied in practice

Evidence examples:
- AI system design records specifying oversight and intervention points
- Training records showing staff awareness of override procedures
- Logs of human review or intervention activity
- Policies defining human-in-the-loop requirements by risk level
- Audit findings confirming oversight controls are operating effectively

Caveat:
This is a learning aid and should be validated against the official ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard and relevant organisational obligations.
