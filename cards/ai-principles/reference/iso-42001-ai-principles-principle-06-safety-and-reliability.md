# Principle-06 Safety and Reliability

Brief explanation:
Safety and reliability mean that AI systems should perform as intended, remain stable under varied conditions, and not cause unintended harm. Organisations should test AI systems thoroughly, monitor their behaviour in production, and have processes in place to respond when systems fail or behave unexpectedly.

Where it fits:
Safety and reliability are foundational to an AI Management System. They ensure that AI systems can be trusted to operate within defined boundaries and that risks of harm are identified and managed across the full system lifecycle.

Practical use:
- Define safety and reliability requirements as part of AI system specifications
- Conduct thorough testing before deployment, including edge cases and failure scenarios
- Monitor AI system performance continuously in production
- Establish incident response processes for AI-related failures or unexpected outputs
- Review safety and reliability controls as part of regular performance evaluation

Evidence examples:
- Test plans and results covering safety and reliability scenarios
- Production monitoring reports with performance metrics
- Incident records for AI system failures and corrective actions taken
- AI system specifications including defined safety thresholds
- Management review records addressing AI system reliability

Caveat:
This is a learning aid and should be validated against the official ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard and relevant organisational obligations.
