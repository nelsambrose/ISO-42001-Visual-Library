# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Clause 7 — Support

---

## Purpose

Clause 7 makes sure the AIMS has the support it needs to work in practice. It covers resources, competence, awareness, communication and documented information.

This clause is about operational enablement. A policy and risk process will not succeed unless people have the right skills, information, tools, communication channels and records.

---

## Sub-clauses

### 7.1 Resources

The organisation must determine and provide the resources needed to establish, implement, maintain and continually improve the AIMS.

Resources may include people, time, budget, tools, data, infrastructure, specialist expertise and access to external advice.

**AI governance resource needs may include:**
- AI governance ownership and coordination
- Risk, legal, privacy, security and compliance support
- Technical expertise in AI system development or evaluation
- Tools for AI inventory, risk assessment and monitoring
- Training and awareness materials
- Incident, issue and nonconformity management processes
- Supplier and third-party assurance capability
- Audit and management review support

A common failure mode is treating AI governance as an extra task for already stretched teams. Clause 7 requires the organisation to make realistic provision for the AIMS to operate.

---

### 7.2 Competence

The organisation must determine the competence needed for people doing work that affects AIMS performance, ensure those people are competent, take action where needed, and retain appropriate evidence.

Competence is not only for technical AI specialists. Anyone whose work affects AI governance may need relevant competence.

**Roles that may need AI-related competence:**

| Role or group | Competence area |
|---|---|
| Senior leaders | AI governance responsibilities, risk appetite and escalation |
| AI system owners | Lifecycle governance, risk assessment and monitoring |
| Developers/data scientists | Responsible AI design, data quality, testing and documentation |
| Product owners | User impact, requirements and release governance |
| Procurement | Third-party AI due diligence and contractual requirements |
| Risk/compliance | AI risk methodology, controls and assurance |
| Customer-facing teams | Appropriate use, limitations and escalation paths |

**Evidence of competence may include:**
- Training records
- Role descriptions
- Skills matrices
- Certifications or professional experience
- Completed workshops
- Assessment results
- Records of mentoring or supervised practice

Training alone is not always enough. Competence means people can apply knowledge effectively in their role.

---

### 7.3 Awareness

People working under the organisation's control must be aware of relevant AIMS matters.

Awareness is broader than competence. It ensures people understand the AI policy, their contribution to the AIMS, the benefits of effective AI management and the consequences of not following AIMS requirements.

**Awareness should cover:**
- The AI policy and responsible AI commitments
- Relevant AI governance procedures
- Individual responsibilities
- How to raise concerns or incidents
- Why controls matter
- The consequences of poor AI governance

**Practical awareness examples:**
- Induction content for new joiners
- Role-based briefings for teams using AI tools
- Guidance for acceptable use of generative AI
- Communication campaigns around AI risk and accountability
- Quick-reference guides for escalation and incident reporting

---

### 7.4 Communication

The organisation must determine the internal and external communications relevant to the AIMS.

Communication planning should identify what needs to be communicated, when, with whom, how and by whom.

**Internal communication may include:**
- AI policy updates
- Changes to approval processes
- Risk assessment requirements
- Incident and escalation routes
- Lessons learned from audits, reviews or nonconformities
- Updates to AI system inventory or monitoring expectations

**External communication may include:**
- Information for customers or users
- Supplier and partner requirements
- Regulatory notifications where applicable
- Public statements about responsible AI commitments
- Information needed by affected interested parties

Poor communication can undermine AI governance quickly. For example, teams may adopt AI tools without understanding approval requirements, data restrictions or monitoring expectations.

---

### 7.5 Documented information

The organisation must control the documented information required by ISO 42001 and the documented information needed for the effectiveness of the AIMS.

Documented information includes both documents and records. Documents describe what should happen. Records provide evidence of what did happen.

**Examples of AIMS documented information:**
- AIMS scope
- AI policy
- AI objectives
- AI system inventory
- AI risk assessment methodology and results
- AI system impact assessments
- Statement of Applicability
- Control implementation evidence
- Competence and awareness records
- Monitoring and measurement results
- Internal audit records
- Management review outputs
- Nonconformity and corrective action records

**Controls for documented information should ensure:**
- Information is available where needed
- It is suitable for use
- It is protected from loss, inappropriate access or unintended change
- Version control is clear
- Review and approval are defined
- Retention and disposal are managed

For AI systems, documentation is especially important because decisions made during design, development, procurement, deployment and monitoring may need to be explained later.

---

## Common audit questions

- What resources have been assigned to operate and improve the AIMS?
- How does the organisation determine required AI governance competence?
- What evidence shows people are competent for their AI-related responsibilities?
- How are staff made aware of AI policy, controls and escalation routes?
- What internal and external AIMS communications have been defined?
- How is documented information created, reviewed, protected and retained?

---

## Key message

Clause 7 gives the AIMS its operating capability. Without resources, competent people, communication and controlled evidence, AI governance remains theoretical.
