# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Clause 10 — Improvement

---

## Purpose

Clause 10 makes continual improvement part of the AIMS. It requires the organisation to respond to nonconformities, take corrective action and improve the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the management system.

This clause is about learning. AI systems, risks, regulations, stakeholders and organisational use cases change quickly. The AIMS must be able to adapt and get better over time.

---

## Sub-clauses

### 10.1 Continual improvement

The organisation must continually improve the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the AIMS.

Continual improvement does not mean constant large-scale change. It means the organisation uses evidence, experience and review outcomes to make the AIMS better over time.

**Improvement inputs may include:**
- Monitoring and measurement results
- Internal audit findings
- Management review decisions
- AI incidents or near misses
- Nonconformities and corrective actions
- Changes in law, regulation or standards
- Changes in AI systems, suppliers or operating context
- Feedback from employees, customers, users or other interested parties
- Lessons learned from testing, validation or post-deployment monitoring
- New risks, opportunities or control weaknesses

**Improvement actions may include:**
- Updating the AI policy or objectives
- Refining the AI risk assessment methodology
- Improving AI system impact assessment templates
- Strengthening approval gates for higher-risk systems
- Updating control implementation evidence
- Improving training and awareness
- Clarifying roles and responsibilities
- Enhancing supplier assurance
- Improving incident escalation and response
- Expanding monitoring for deployed AI systems

The aim is not perfection on day one. The aim is a management system that detects weakness and improves in a controlled, evidence-based way.

---

### 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action

When a nonconformity occurs, the organisation must react to it, control and correct it where applicable, deal with consequences, evaluate the need for action to eliminate the cause, implement needed action, review effectiveness and update the AIMS if necessary.

A nonconformity is a failure to meet a requirement. The requirement may come from ISO 42001, the organisation's own AIMS processes, legal obligations, contractual commitments or selected controls.

**Examples of AI governance nonconformities:**
- An in-scope AI system is missing from the inventory
- An AI risk assessment was not completed before deployment
- A selected Annex A control is not implemented
- The Statement of Applicability is incomplete or out of date
- Staff cannot demonstrate required competence for assigned responsibilities
- AI system monitoring is not performed as planned
- A supplier AI due diligence step was skipped
- A management review action remains open without justification
- Documented information is uncontrolled or inconsistent

**Corrective action should show:**

| Step | Evidence |
|---|---|
| React | Immediate containment or correction |
| Evaluate cause | Root cause analysis or reasoned assessment |
| Act | Corrective action plan and owners |
| Review | Evidence that action was effective |
| Update | Changes to process, controls or documentation where needed |

Corrective action is different from simple correction. Correction fixes the immediate issue. Corrective action addresses the cause so the issue is less likely to recur.

## Improvement mindset for AI governance

AI governance improves when the organisation treats findings, incidents and feedback as learning signals rather than administrative failures.

**Useful improvement questions:**
- Did this issue reveal a weak process, unclear role or missing control?
- Could the same problem exist in other AI systems or teams?
- Was the risk assessment method good enough to detect the issue earlier?
- Were people aware of the requirement?
- Was the requirement realistic and properly resourced?
- Should monitoring, escalation or approval criteria change?
- Does the issue affect the AIMS scope, policy, objectives or Statement of Applicability?

Improvement should be proportionate. Not every issue requires a major redesign, but repeated minor issues may reveal a systemic weakness.

---

## Common audit questions

- How does the organisation identify improvement opportunities for the AIMS?
- What nonconformities have been identified, and how were they handled?
- Can corrective actions be traced from finding to root cause to closure?
- How does the organisation check whether corrective actions were effective?
- What changes have been made to the AIMS based on audit findings, incidents or management review?
- How are lessons learned shared across relevant teams?

## Key message

Clause 10 closes the loop. It ensures the AIMS does not stand still while AI systems, risks and expectations keep changing.
