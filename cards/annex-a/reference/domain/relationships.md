# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Domain — Relationships

---

## Purpose

The Relationships domain governs how the organisation manages AI-related obligations with third parties, customers, and other stakeholders who develop, supply, deploy, or are affected by AI systems.

AI systems rarely exist in isolation. They depend on data from suppliers, infrastructure from cloud providers, and models from third-party vendors. The Relationships domain ensures those dependencies are governed, not assumed.

---

## Controls in this domain

Relationships spans Annex A controls **A.8**, **A.9**, and **A.10**.

### A.8 — Information for Interested Parties

The organisation must provide appropriate information about AI systems to relevant stakeholders. This includes transparency about how AI is used, what decisions it influences, and what recourse is available. The level of disclosure should be proportionate to the impact of the system.

### A.9 — Use of AI Systems

Where AI systems are used (rather than developed), the organisation must ensure that use is within the intended purpose, that appropriate controls are applied, and that human oversight is maintained where required. Organisations cannot transfer accountability simply by using a third-party AI system.

### A.10 — Third-party and Customer Relationships

The organisation must manage AI-related obligations with third parties and customers. This includes supplier due diligence, contractual requirements, and ensuring that AI systems provided to customers are fit for purpose and responsibly governed.

---

## Why Relationships matter

Most organisations are simultaneously AI developers, AI users, and AI suppliers. The Relationships domain ensures that accountability does not fall through the gaps between those roles.

Auditors look closely at A.10 because supply chain failures and undisclosed AI use are among the most common real-world sources of AI harm.

---

## Common audit questions

- How does the organisation communicate AI system information to relevant stakeholders?
- Are AI systems being used within their intended purpose?
- What due diligence does the organisation perform on AI suppliers?
- How are AI-related obligations to customers documented and managed?
