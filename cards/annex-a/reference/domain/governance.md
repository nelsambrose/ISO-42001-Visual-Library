# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Domain — Governance

---

## Purpose

The Governance domain establishes the policies, leadership accountability, and organisational direction needed for responsible AI management. It sets the tone from the top and ensures that AI-related commitments are documented, communicated and owned.

Without Governance controls, an organisation may develop or deploy AI without clear principles, defined accountability, or a shared understanding of what responsible AI means in practice.

---

## Controls in this domain

Governance spans Annex A controls **A.2** and **A.3**.

### A.2 — Policies Related to AI

The organisation must establish AI-related policies covering intended use, ethics principles, risk appetite and acceptable behaviour of AI systems. Policies must be approved by appropriate authority, communicated to relevant parties, and reviewed regularly.

### A.3 — Internal Organisation

The organisation must define roles, responsibilities and authorities for AI governance. This includes who is accountable for AI decisions, who manages AI-related risks, and how AI governance connects to existing management structures.

---

## Why Governance comes first

Governance controls are foundational. Without policies (A.2), there is no shared baseline for what the organisation is trying to achieve. Without clear internal organisation (A.3), there is no accountability to enforce it.

Auditors typically examine Governance first because weaknesses here cascade through every other domain.

---

## Common audit questions

- Does the organisation have documented AI policies, and who approved them?
- How are AI policies communicated to staff and relevant stakeholders?
- Are roles and responsibilities for AI governance clearly defined and assigned?
- How does AI governance connect to senior leadership and board oversight?
