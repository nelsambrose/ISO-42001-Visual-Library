# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Domain — Organisation

---

## Purpose

The Organisation domain ensures the right people, skills, and resources are in place to manage AI responsibly. It translates Governance-level policy into operational capability: who does what, with what competence, and supported by what resources.

---

## Controls in this domain

Organisation spans Annex A control **A.4**.

### A.4 — Resources for AI Systems

The organisation must ensure sufficient resources are provided for the AIMS. This includes human resources, technical infrastructure, and the financial investment needed to develop, deploy, monitor and maintain AI systems responsibly.

Resources must be appropriate for the scale and risk profile of the organisation's AI activities. Claiming to manage AI responsibly while under-resourcing the people and tools required is a common audit gap.

**Key resource considerations:**
- Competent staff with AI-related roles and responsibilities (linked to A.3)
- Access to expertise in AI ethics, security, data management, and legal compliance
- Technical infrastructure for monitoring, testing, and auditing AI systems
- Time and budget allocated for impact assessments and risk reviews

---

## Why Organisation matters

Policies and processes only work if the people responsible for them have the time, skill, and support to carry them out. The Organisation domain is the bridge between intention and execution.

---

## Common audit questions

- What resources has the organisation allocated to AI governance?
- Are staff with AI responsibilities competent to perform them?
- How are competency gaps identified and addressed?
- Is there sufficient technical infrastructure to support responsible AI operation?
