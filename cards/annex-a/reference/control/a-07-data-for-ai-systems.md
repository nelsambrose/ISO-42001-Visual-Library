# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.7 — Data for AI Systems

---

## Purpose

A.7 requires the organisation to manage data used in AI systems responsibly. Data is the foundation of AI performance — poor quality, biased, or inappropriately sourced data leads directly to unreliable or harmful AI outputs.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must apply governance to data used for training, testing, and operating AI systems:

**Data quality:**
- Assess and document the quality of data used in AI systems
- Identify and manage issues with completeness, accuracy, consistency, and relevance
- Ensure data is representative of the population or context the AI system will operate in

**Data provenance:**
- Know where data comes from and how it was collected
- Assess whether data was collected ethically and with appropriate consent
- Document data lineage so that issues can be traced back to their source

**Data appropriateness:**
- Ensure data is appropriate for the intended use of the AI system
- Identify and mitigate bias in training data
- Avoid using data in ways that are inconsistent with how it was collected

**Data governance:**
- Apply data protection obligations where personal data is involved
- Define retention and deletion schedules for AI-related data
- Restrict access to sensitive training data

---

## Why it matters

An AI system is only as trustworthy as the data it was built on. Poor data governance is one of the leading causes of biased, inaccurate, or harmful AI outputs — and one of the hardest problems to fix after deployment.

---

## Common audit questions

- How does the organisation assess data quality for AI systems?
- Is data provenance documented for training and test datasets?
- How is bias in training data identified and managed?
- Are data protection obligations applied to personal data used in AI systems?
- What controls govern access to and retention of AI training data?
