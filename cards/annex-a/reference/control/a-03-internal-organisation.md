# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.3 — Internal Organisation

---

## Purpose

A.3 requires the organisation to define and assign roles, responsibilities, and authorities for AI governance. It ensures that someone is accountable for every significant AI decision and that AI governance is not treated as a shared but unowned responsibility.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must:

- Define roles and responsibilities for AI-related activities, including who is accountable for individual AI systems
- Establish how AI governance decisions are made and escalated
- Ensure AI responsibilities are communicated to those who hold them
- Integrate AI governance roles into existing management structures where appropriate

**Key roles to consider:**
- Who owns the AI policy?
- Who is responsible for impact assessments?
- Who approves deployment of new AI systems?
- Who monitors AI systems in operation?
- Who is accountable when things go wrong?

Roles may be new or assigned to existing positions, but they must be explicitly defined — not assumed.

---

## Why it matters

In many organisations, responsibility for AI is diffuse. Technology teams build systems, procurement teams buy them, and business teams deploy them — with no single point of accountability. A.3 closes that gap.

Without defined roles, controls cannot be consistently implemented, reviewed, or improved. Accountability is the prerequisite for everything else in the AIMS.

---

## Common audit questions

- Are AI governance roles and responsibilities formally defined and documented?
- Do the people in those roles know they are responsible?
- How is AI governance accountability escalated to senior leadership?
- Is there a clear owner for each AI system in scope of the AIMS?
