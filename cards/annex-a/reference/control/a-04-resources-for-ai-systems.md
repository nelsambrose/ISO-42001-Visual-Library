# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.4 — Resources for AI Systems

---

## Purpose

A.4 requires the organisation to ensure that sufficient and appropriate resources are provided to support the development, deployment, operation, and governance of AI systems. Responsible AI management cannot happen without the people, tools, and investment to make it real.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must identify and provide resources appropriate to its AI activities, including:

**Human resources:**
- Staff with the skills and competence to govern, develop, deploy, and monitor AI systems
- Access to expertise in AI ethics, data management, cybersecurity, and legal compliance
- Sufficient capacity — people cannot manage AI risk effectively if it is treated as a side task

**Technical resources:**
- Infrastructure to develop and test AI systems safely
- Tools for monitoring AI systems in operation
- Systems for logging, auditing, and reviewing AI-related decisions and outputs

**Financial resources:**
- Budget allocated for ongoing AI governance activities, not just development
- Investment in training, tooling, and external expertise where internal capability is limited

Resources must be proportionate to the scale and risk profile of the organisation's AI activities.

---

## Why it matters

Under-resourcing AI governance is one of the most common reasons AIMS controls fail in practice. Policies exist, roles are assigned, but without adequate time, tools, and budget, controls cannot be consistently operated or improved.

---

## Common audit questions

- What resources has the organisation allocated to AI governance?
- Are staff responsible for AI governance competent and available to fulfil their roles?
- Is there a budget line for AI governance, or is it absorbed informally?
- Does the organisation have the technical tools needed to monitor and audit AI systems?
