# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.10 — Third-party and Customer Relationships

---

## Purpose

A.10 requires the organisation to manage AI-related obligations with third parties and customers. This includes supplier due diligence, contractual requirements, and ensuring that AI systems provided to customers are fit for purpose and responsibly governed.

---

## What the control requires

**Third-party (supplier) relationships:**
- Identify third parties involved in the organisation's AI activities (e.g. AI model providers, data suppliers, cloud infrastructure providers)
- Conduct due diligence on third parties' AI governance practices where relevant
- Include AI-related obligations in contracts with suppliers (e.g. data quality, bias management, incident notification)
- Monitor supplier performance against AI-related obligations

**Customer relationships:**
- Be transparent with customers about AI systems used to serve them (see also A.8)
- Define and document the organisation's obligations to customers regarding AI system behaviour, limitations, and recourse
- Ensure that AI systems provided to customers are fit for their intended purpose
- Manage customer complaints or concerns related to AI system behaviour

**Supply chain accountability:**
- The organisation remains responsible for the behaviour of AI systems it deploys, even where components are sourced from third parties
- Third-party AI models or data cannot be used as a shield against accountability

---

## Why it matters

AI systems increasingly depend on complex supply chains — third-party models, externally sourced data, and cloud-hosted infrastructure. Without governance of these relationships, the organisation cannot fully understand or manage the risks of its own AI systems.

Auditors pay close attention to A.10 because supply chain failures and inadequate customer transparency are among the most common sources of real-world AI harm.

---

## Common audit questions

- Has the organisation identified third parties involved in its AI activities?
- Are AI-related obligations included in supplier contracts?
- How does the organisation monitor third-party AI-related performance?
- What obligations does the organisation have to customers regarding AI systems, and are these documented?
- How are customer complaints about AI system behaviour managed?
