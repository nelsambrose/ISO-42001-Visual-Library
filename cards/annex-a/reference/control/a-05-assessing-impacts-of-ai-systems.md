# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.5 — Assessing Impacts of AI Systems

---

## Purpose

A.5 requires the organisation to assess the potential impacts of AI systems — including intended benefits and unintended harms — before deployment and throughout the system's lifecycle. Impact assessment is the mechanism by which AI risk is made visible and manageable.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must carry out impact assessments for AI systems that consider:

**Potential benefits:**
- The intended purpose and value delivered by the system
- Who benefits and how

**Potential harms:**
- Harms to individuals (e.g. unfair decisions, privacy violations, physical harm)
- Harms to groups (e.g. discriminatory outcomes, exclusion)
- Harms to society (e.g. erosion of trust, amplification of misinformation)
- Harms to the organisation (e.g. reputational, legal, financial)

**Assessment criteria:**
- The likelihood that harm occurs
- The severity and reversibility of potential harm
- The breadth of impact (how many people affected)
- The vulnerability of affected parties

Impact assessments must be:
- Proportionate to the risk profile of the AI system
- Documented as documented information
- Reviewed when the system, its context, or its use changes significantly

---

## Why it matters

Without structured impact assessment, high-risk AI systems can be deployed based on performance alone, with harmful side effects discovered only after harm has occurred. A.5 makes impact assessment a prerequisite for deployment, not an afterthought.

---

## Common audit questions

- Does the organisation have a process for assessing AI system impacts?
- Who is responsible for conducting impact assessments?
- Are assessments documented, and are records retained?
- How are high-impact findings acted upon before deployment?
- When are assessments reviewed or repeated?
