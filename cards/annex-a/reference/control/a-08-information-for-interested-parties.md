# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.8 — Information for Interested Parties

---

## Purpose

A.8 requires the organisation to provide appropriate information about AI systems to relevant stakeholders. Transparency about how AI works, what decisions it influences, and what recourse exists is a key component of responsible AI governance.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must determine what information to provide, to whom, and through what channels:

**What to communicate:**
- The intended purpose and scope of AI systems
- How AI systems influence decisions that affect stakeholders
- Known limitations, risks, or uncertainties of the AI system
- What human oversight or review is available
- How stakeholders can raise concerns or seek redress

**To whom:**
- Customers or users directly affected by AI decisions
- Employees subject to AI-based management or monitoring tools
- Regulators and supervisory authorities as required
- Other interested parties identified under Clause 4.2

**Proportionality:**
The level of disclosure should be proportionate to the impact of the AI system. High-impact systems affecting individual rights or safety require more transparency than low-risk internal tools.

---

## Why it matters

Trust in AI depends on transparency. Stakeholders who are affected by AI decisions — but have no visibility into how those decisions are made — cannot exercise meaningful oversight or seek appropriate redress. A.8 makes transparency an organisational obligation, not a communications choice.

---

## Common audit questions

- Has the organisation identified which stakeholders need information about its AI systems?
- What information is provided, and through what channels?
- Is the level of disclosure proportionate to the impact of the system?
- Are stakeholders informed of how to raise concerns or seek redress?
