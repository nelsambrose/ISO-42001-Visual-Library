# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.2 — Policies Related to AI

---

## Purpose

A.2 requires the organisation to establish and maintain policies that define its approach to responsible AI. Policies are the written foundation for everything else — they define what the organisation stands for, what is permitted, and what is not.

---

## What the control requires

The organisation must establish AI-related policies that address:

- The organisation's intended approach to developing, deploying, or using AI systems
- AI ethics principles and values the organisation commits to
- Risk appetite for AI-related harms and unintended outcomes
- Acceptable and unacceptable uses of AI within the organisation
- Responsibilities for compliance with the policy

Policies must be:
- **Approved** by appropriate authority (typically senior leadership)
- **Communicated** to staff and relevant stakeholders
- **Available** as documented information
- **Reviewed** at planned intervals or when significant changes occur

---

## Why it matters

Without a documented AI policy, there is no shared definition of what responsible AI means for the organisation. Individual teams and systems operate without a common standard, making consistent governance impossible.

An AI policy is not a once-written document. It must evolve as the organisation's AI activities, regulatory environment, and risk landscape change.

---

## Common audit questions

- Does the organisation have a documented AI policy or set of policies?
- Who approved the policy, and when was it last reviewed?
- How is the policy communicated to staff with AI-related roles?
- Does the policy address ethics, risk appetite, and acceptable use?
- Is the policy available to relevant external parties where appropriate?
