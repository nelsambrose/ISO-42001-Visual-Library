# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Annex A Control A.9 — Use of AI Systems

---

## Purpose

A.9 requires the organisation to ensure that AI systems are used within their intended purpose and that appropriate controls are applied when doing so. Organisations that use third-party AI systems — rather than build their own — cannot transfer accountability simply by pointing to the supplier.

---

## What the control requires

When an organisation uses AI systems (whether built internally or acquired externally), it must:

**Use within intended purpose:**
- Understand the intended purpose and limitations of the AI system
- Ensure the system is used only for the purposes it was designed and validated for
- Avoid applying the system to contexts where it has not been assessed

**Human oversight:**
- Determine where human review of AI outputs is required
- Implement mechanisms for humans to review, question, or override AI decisions
- Ensure that automation bias — over-reliance on AI outputs — is actively managed

**Controls for use:**
- Define acceptable use conditions for the AI system
- Apply access controls to limit who can use the system and for what purposes
- Monitor actual use to detect deviation from intended use

**User awareness:**
- Ensure staff using AI systems understand what the system does, its limitations, and when to escalate or override

---

## Why it matters

Many organisations deploy AI systems without fully understanding their intended scope, limitations, or failure modes. When AI is used outside its intended purpose, or without adequate human oversight, the risk of harm increases significantly — and the organisation remains accountable.

---

## Common audit questions

- Does the organisation understand the intended purpose of the AI systems it uses?
- Are there controls ensuring AI systems are used within that intended purpose?
- Where is human oversight required, and is it implemented in practice?
- Are users of AI systems trained to understand limitations and escalation paths?
