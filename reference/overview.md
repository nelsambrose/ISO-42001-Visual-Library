# ISO 42001 Reference Card: Overview

**ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management Systems**

---

## What it is

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving an **AI Management System (AIMS)**. Published in December 2023, it is the world's first certifiable standard specifically for AI governance.

It is a **governance standard**, not a technical standard. It does not tell you how to build AI. It tells you how to govern it.

---

## Who it is for

- Organisations that **develop** AI systems
- Organisations that **deploy** AI systems
- Organisations that **use** AI systems provided by third parties
- Any size of organisation, any sector

The standard is scalable. Proportionality is built into its design: controls and obligations are calibrated to the nature, scale and context of the organisation's AI activities.

---

## Structure

The standard follows the **ISO High Level Structure (HLS)**, also used by ISO 27001 (information security) and ISO 9001 (quality management). This means it can be integrated with existing management systems.

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Clauses 1–3 | Scope, normative references, terms and definitions |
| Clause 4 | Context of the Organisation |
| Clause 5 | Leadership |
| Clause 6 | Planning |
| Clause 7 | Support |
| Clause 8 | Operation |
| Clause 9 | Performance Evaluation |
| Clause 10 | Improvement |
| Annex A | Normative controls (38 controls across 9 categories) |
| Annex B | AI objectives and guidance |
| Annex C | Guidance on AI risk assessment |
| Annex D | Use of ISO 42001 for AI system providers and customers |

---

## The PDCA cycle

ISO 42001 is structured around the **Plan–Do–Check–Act** cycle:

```
PLAN  →  Understand context, set objectives, assess risks
  DO  →  Implement controls, operate the AIMS
CHECK →  Monitor, measure, audit, review
  ACT →  Improve, correct nonconformities
```

This is not a linear process. It is a continual loop that drives ongoing improvement.

---

## Key concepts

### AI system
An engineered system that generates outputs such as predictions, recommendations, decisions or content that influences real or virtual environments.

### AI management system (AIMS)
The policies, processes, roles and documented information an organisation uses to govern its AI activities.

### AI risk
Combination of the probability of an AI-related event and the severity of the consequences to individuals, groups or society.

### Interested parties
Anyone who can affect or be affected by the organisation's AI activities: employees, customers, regulators, affected communities.

## The 38 Annex A controls

Annex A is normative, meaning selected controls become mandatory as part of the Statement of Applicability. Controls are grouped into 9 categories:

| # | Category |
|---|---|
| A.2 | Policies related to AI |
| A.3 | Internal organisation |
| A.4 | Resources for AI systems |
| A.5 | Assessing AI system impacts |
| A.6 | AI system life cycle |
| A.7 | Data for AI systems |
| A.8 | Information for interested parties of AI systems |
| A.9 | Use of AI systems |
| A.10 | Third-party and customer relationships |

## Key benefits

| Benefit | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| Structured governance | A repeatable framework instead of ad hoc decisions |
| Certifiable | Third-party certification provides external assurance |
| Risk-based | Controls are applied proportionally to actual risk |
| Integrable | Designed to work alongside ISO 27001 and ISO 9001 |
| Internationally recognised | Credible to regulators, customers and partners |
| Regulatory alignment | Strong overlap with EU AI Act requirements |

---

## What certification requires

1. Implement an AIMS that meets all normative requirements
2. Produce a **Statement of Applicability** covering Annex A controls
3. Pass a **Stage 1 audit** (documentation review)
4. Pass a **Stage 2 audit** (operational effectiveness)
5. Maintain through **annual surveillance audits**
6. Recertify every **3 years**

---

## What it does not cover

- How to build or train AI models (that is for technical standards)
- Product safety certification for AI systems
- Specific sectoral requirements (healthcare, finance, etc.)
- Compliance with specific laws (though it supports regulatory alignment)
